Alright, so here's the thing about building tools for yourself. You spend all this time getting the transcription working, and then you realize the bottleneck isn't even the model anymore, it's all the stuff around it. Like the time it takes from pressing the hotkey to actually starting the recording, and then from stopping to getting the text pasted. That's the bit that matters. Because when you're in flow and you just want to capture a thought, every second of latency pulls you out of it. So yeah, the goal here is to make it feel instant. Press the button, talk, press it again, and the text is just there. No waiting, no spinning, just done. And the quality has to be good enough that you don't need to go back and fix it every time. That's the whole point.

The other thing I've been thinking about is how you handle the boundaries between segments. Like when you're recording for thirty seconds or a minute, the system has to chop that audio into pieces and transcribe each one separately. And the problem is, if you cut in the middle of a sentence, whisper doesn't have enough context to get it right. So you end up with weird artifacts at the edges, like repeated words or sentences that just trail off into nothing. The fix is to use longer segments, give the model more context, and then clean up the boundaries afterwards. It's the same principle as anything else in engineering, right? You optimize for the common case and handle the edge cases gracefully.

And then there's the microphone quality thing. I've been testing with the built-in MacBook mic this whole time, which is honestly not great when you're sitting a meter away from it. The USB condenser mic should be way better for this. Closer to the mouth, better signal to noise ratio, less room echo. All of that feeds directly into transcription accuracy because whisper is basically just predicting tokens from audio features. Better audio in, better text out. It's that simple. So let's see how much of a difference the mic actually makes. That's what this test is for.
